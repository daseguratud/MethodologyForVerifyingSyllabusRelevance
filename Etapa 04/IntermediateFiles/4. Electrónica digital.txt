The technological development of recent years is largely due to the advance in the conception of modern techniques in the design and implementation of electronic circuits among
which digital systems stand out as raw material for the majority of electronic devices or components used at the home, business or academic level.
academic space provides a basic foundation on the analysis of basic digital circuits such as combinational and sequential circuits, which are necessary
for the interpretation, analysis and development of more complex digital circuits such as processors, controllers or integrated circuits for specific applications.
Additionally, digital circuit synthesis techniques are introduced along with design procedures seen from different levels of abstraction, which provides a
complete vision to address hardware development problems using various technological options
general objective
develop in the student skills and abilities in the analysis, design and implementation of digital systems applicable in computing, data processing,
industrial instrumentation and control
specific objectives
provide the student with the basic concepts of Boolean algebra for the analysis and synthesis of combinational circuits
familiarize the student with the concept of memory and its conception at a hardware level for the development of sequential circuits
give the student knowledge about the hardware implementation of finite state machines
show an overview of the applications in which microprocessor technologies are used, dsp's and fpga's microcontrollers, justification of the academic space
